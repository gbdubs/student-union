News

Your representatives are hard at work. Here is what they have been up to!

Reps to the Board of Trustees Release Survey Results
August 28th, 2015

Emily Conrad '17 and Grady Ward '16 Announced the survey results in an email to the student body, detailing the findings of their surveys and interviews with undergraduates, faculty, administrators and trustees. The report focused on tangible changes that the University could make to better support its students and improve the optics of its financial aid policies, with the end goal of supporting the emotional and financial wellbeing of students as best as possible. The 36-page report was heavily discussed in the campus papers, and will serve as a jumping off point from which the Board of Trustees will thoroughly examine financial policy at the beginning of the next year.

Aboard Changes Imminent, Announced at Press Conference
August 30th, 2015

President Nyah Macklin emailed the student body on Wednesday to announce a press conference about the challenges that the A-Board has faced this year, and to discuss the potential for structural changes to prevent similar mistakes and inconveniences in the future. Macklin's discerussion at the press conference has now been proposed as a constitutional ammendment that would restructure A-Board to an eleven member body much more like the Senate. More details on the proposal can be found in the campus wide email describing the Changes.

Student Union Website
November 4th, 2015

The student union website launched this week, beginning a complete overhaul of how the union communicates with the student body. At the forefront of its design and implementation were President Nyah Macklin, The director of communications Naya, and a web developer with Union ties, Grady Ward. Time will tell how this new look will fit in to Brandeis' network of online student resources, and the Union invites any and all feedback as they continue to work on the site. Additionally, there is currently an opening for an executive position to maintain and update the site. No technical experience is required, please contact Nyah Macklin if you are interested.
